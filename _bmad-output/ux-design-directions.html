Chapter 1: The Descent

The air in the subterranean corridor was thick with the scent of damp stone and old bronze. Elara pressed her palm against the wall, her fingers tracing the intricate carvings that shouldn't have been there. She remembered the map—the one her father had left behind—and it clearly showed a straight path to the central chamber. But here, the corridor spiraled down into a darkness that felt deliberate.

She adjusted her lantern, the light catching the glint of blue in her eyes. "Her sapphire gaze scanned the ancient runic script for any sign of a trap." It was a subtle detail, but one that grounded her in the moment. She took a step forward, the sound of her boot clicking against the floor echoing in the silence.

Something felt wrong. The inscriptions weren't just decorative; they were a warning. A warning about the consistency of her own memories. Had the eyes of the statues always been closed?
